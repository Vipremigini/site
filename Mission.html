Mission

VikramSat - 1

  • Global IoT network
  • Student scientific payloads
  • StarGate 
To further expand on our mission, we aim to design, develop and deploy a Low Earth Orbit (LEO) CubeSat satellite that utilizes LoRaWAN technology to establish a global IoT network. Our satellite will provide a low-cost, low-power and long-range communication solution that can be used in remote and under-served areas where traditional communication methods are limited or unavailable. This network will allow for the collection and transmission of data from a variety of sources including environmental monitoring sensors, industrial automation systems, and agricultural equipment.

One of the key focuses of our mission is to promote education and research in the field of space technology and IoT. We aim to provide educational opportunities for students and researchers to participate in the design, development, and deployment of our CubeSat, which will provide hands-on experience in the field. Additionally, our CubeSat will serve as a test-bed for new IoT technologies, which will be valuable for researchers and industry professionals alike.

In addition to providing educational opportunities, our CubeSat will also have a significant impact on the environment, industry and agriculture. By providing real-time data from remote areas, we will be able to monitor and track changes in the environment, which will aid in conservation efforts. In the industrial sector, our network will allow for the remote monitoring and control of equipment, leading to increased efficiency and cost savings. In agriculture, our network will provide farmers with real-time information about crop growth and weather conditions, which will allow them to make more informed decisions about planting, harvesting, and pest control.

Overall, our mission is to establish a global IoT network based on LoRaWAN technology that will provide low-cost, efficient communication solutions in remote and under-served areas while promoting education and research in the field of space technology and IoT.

Our mission includes a strong focus on providing educational opportunities for students from across the country. As part of this, we will be selecting a group of students to actively participate in the design and development of advanced scientific payloads for our CubeSat. These payloads will be used to observe and analyze various scientific parameters in space, such as atmospheric conditions and radiation levels.

This initiative will give students the opportunity to gain hands-on experience in designing and developing scientific payloads for space applications. They will have the chance to work with cutting-edge technology and learn about the unique challenges and considerations that come with designing payloads for space missions.

The payloads will be designed to collect a wide range of data and perform various scientific experiments in order to answer research questions in the field of environmental monitoring, industrial automation, and agriculture. For example, environmental payloads will measure atmospheric pressure, temperature, humidity and radiation levels, which will provide valuable information for scientists and researchers working in the field.

Overall, our mission is not just to establish a global IoT network and launch a CubeSat, but also to provide educational opportunities for students and to promote research in the field of space technology and IoT. We believe that by providing students with the chance to design and develop advanced scientific payloads, we will be helping to cultivate the next generation of scientists, engineers, and researchers.

Our mission is to develop a highly efficient satellite architecture, called StarGate, that will be capable of providing next-generation performance and cost-efficiency for space missions. The StarGate architecture will allow for the development of more reliable, cost-effective, and powerful satellites that can provide a wide range of services, including communication, navigation, Earth observation, and scientific research.

The StarGate architecture will be designed with a focus on modularity, scalability, and flexibility. This will enable it to be easily adapted to a variety of mission requirements, and will allow for the integration of new technologies as they become available. Additionally, the architecture will be optimized for low power consumption, which will allow for the use of smaller and more affordable launch vehicles.

One of the key advantages of the StarGate architecture is its ability to provide a high performance to cost ratio. This will enable the development of satellites that can provide more capabilities and services, while still being affordable to launch and operate. This will pave the way for more commercial and scientific missions, which will benefit a wide range of industries, including telecommunications, navigation, Earth observation, weather forecasting, and scientific research.

Overall, our mission is to develop a next-generation satellite architecture that will enable the development of more reliable, cost-effective, and powerful satellites that can provide a wide range of services. The StarGate architecture will provide a high performance to cost ratio, which will pave the way for more commercial and scientific missions.

VikramSat - 2

  • Deep space missions
  • Space based energy
  • High efficiency Remote Sensing

Our mission is to revolutionize deep space communication by developing a cutting-edge laser communication system with a high data rate. This system will allow for faster, more efficient transmission of data between spacecraft and ground control, enabling us to push the boundaries of what is currently possible in deep space exploration.

Our laser communication system will be specifically designed for deep space missions, taking into account the unique challenges presented by long distances and the vacuum of space. By using laser technology, we will be able to achieve data rates that are orders of magnitude higher than traditional radio frequency-based systems, which will enable us to transmit large amounts of scientific data and images quickly and accurately.

Our satellite will also serve as a testbed for new technologies that could be used in future space exploration missions. By developing a high data rate laser communication system, we will be able to demonstrate the feasibility of this technology and pave the way for its use in future missions.

Our goal is to change the way we think about deep space communication and to push the boundaries of what is currently possible. With our high data rate laser communication system, we will be able to unlock new possibilities for deep space exploration and open up new frontiers in scientific discovery. We are excited to be leading the way in this field and are committed to making this vision a reality.

Our mission is to revolutionize the way we think about and use energy. With the increasing demand for energy sources that are renewable and sustainable, we recognize the need to explore new and innovative methods of energy production.

With this in mind, we aim to test the feasibility of space-based power, which holds the potential to provide a clean and unlimited source of energy for our planet. Our goal is to demonstrate the viability of this technology and to lay the groundwork for a more sustainable future.

We believe that space-based power has the potential to address many of the world's energy challenges, including reducing greenhouse gas emissions, decreasing reliance on finite resources, and providing energy to remote and underserved regions.

By pioneering the development and testing of space-based power systems, we hope to lead the way towards a cleaner, greener, and more sustainable energy future for all.

Our mission is to address the increasing need for monitoring Earth from space by developing and deploying more efficient, economical, and fast remote sensing systems. This is becoming increasingly important as we continue to face challenges such as climate change and natural disasters. To achieve this goal, we will be utilizing cutting-edge technologies in the form of revolutionary monolithic telescope design and multi-spectral imaging systems.

Our revolutionary monolithic telescope design will allow us to achieve a higher level of precision and accuracy in our imaging systems, which is crucial for monitoring and understanding the Earth's environment. This technology will enable us to capture more detailed and accurate images of the Earth's surface, which will be invaluable for a wide range of applications such as environmental monitoring, natural resource management, and disaster response.

In addition to the revolutionary monolithic telescope design, we will be incorporating multi-spectral imaging systems into our satellites. This will allow us to gather a wide range of information about the Earth's surface, including data on vegetation, water bodies, and minerals. This information will be crucial for a wide range of applications such as environmental monitoring, natural resource management, and agricultural planning.

One of the key advantages of our approach is that our satellites can be deployed on short notice. This means that we can respond quickly to natural disasters, such as hurricanes or earthquakes, and provide critical information to aid in rescue and recovery efforts. Furthermore, our satellites are designed to be economical and efficient, which allows us to gather more data over a longer period of time and at a lower cost than traditional remote sensing systems.

In summary, our mission is to change the way we monitor and understand the Earth by deploying innovative remote sensing systems. By utilizing revolutionary monolithic telescope technology and multi-spectral imaging systems, we are able to achieve a higher level of precision and accuracy, respond quickly to natural disasters, and gather more data at a lower cost. This advancement will have a significant impact in various domains such as environmental monitoring, natural resource management, disaster response, and more.
